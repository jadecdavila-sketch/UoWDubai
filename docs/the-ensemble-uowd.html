Aurica · Composed for the University of Wollongong in Dubai

A course is not generated.
It is conducted.

Generative courses, built the way Claude Code was built: the model is the instrument. The teaching craft wrapped around it is the moat. This is the council that carries the craft.

◆ A working draft, composed to be argued with

One faculty deck becomes one score: chunks as measures, each carrying what must land, what must be avoided, and how we will know. The score is canonical. The performances are not.

I.
The Thesis

Pedagogy first.
Personalization is earned.

Every voice in the room serves one sentence, and when a voice's best practice collides with it, the sentence wins.

◆ Draft thesis · for your reaction
The council encodes the instructional craft. The faculty member conducts the ensemble. The student conducts the performance. The score is the contract between them.
Fully personalized, generated-on-the-fly courses collide with what learning science knows about structure and shared reference points. So the system earns personalization the disciplined way: pedagogy is encoded and faculty-approved first, and the personal arrives as a performance of that approved score, never as a replacement for it.

The long vision is yours: courses that meet each student where they are, in their language, through their world. The discipline is ours to encode: that vision only holds if the teaching underneath it is structurally sound. Trust, but verify, built into the architecture rather than appended to it.

III.
The Rehearsal

The deck goes in.
The score comes out.

Tightening the harness: drafting voices propose, evaluating voices push back with named gaps, and the loop runs until the room is satisfied or the disagreement is worth showing you.

1
The deck arrives
Your slides become the data room. Everything downstream is accountable to them.
2
The drafters play
The Practitioner shapes the teaching; the Scriptwright gives it a human voice, chunk by chunk.
3
The room pushes back
Each evaluating voice critiques through its own lens, with named gaps. A failed check sends the chunk back to step 2.
4
The Concertmaster weaves
Agreements, collisions, and open calls are synthesized into the score and its reasoning trace.
5
You conduct
Approve, redirect, or turn a voice up and send it back to rehearsal. The judgment is yours, with the room's reasoning open beneath it.

The trace is not an afterthought. Like the deal scorecard that came before it, every script ships with its making-of: where the voices agreed, where they pulled apart, and which calls were left to you.

IV.
The Score

What is locked,
and what is free.

Each chunk carries its invariants: the concepts that must land, the misconceptions that must not, and the checkpoint that proves it. Everything else is performance.

Chunk 3 · Escalation of Commitment
From a Principles of Management score
Must land
Commitment escalates because of identity and self-justification, not arithmetic. The money is gone either way.
Must avoid
The misconception that sunk cost is only about money. Time, reputation, and public position sink too.
Checkpoint
The learner names one commitment in their own work or studies that deserves the test.
◆ Yours
Which example carries it, how hard the question lands, and whether this chunk is the place to be bold.

This is the answer to the hardest question in the room: how can a thousand students each get their own lecture without the course dissolving into a thousand courses? Because the skeleton is shared. The invariants are the reference points every student holds in common, whatever costume the concept wore when it arrived.

The performer, and the interlude

The performance has a face. The Performer is the talking-head avatar that delivers each chunk and, when the student raises a hand, stops. A question opens an interlude: a bounded conversation inside the chunk, played by the same performer under the same rules. Its answers trace to the score and the deck or wear the enrichment tag, and the invariants ride along, compiled into the performer's instructions, because the full council cannot convene on every spoken turn. The Performer is not a seat at the table. It is the instrument the score is played through.

When a question runs past the edge of the score, the performer says so plainly, and the question is routed to a ledger on the podium. That ledger may be the most valuable artifact in the system: the question every third student asks is the score's best critic, and in Phase III it walks back upstream into the rehearsal room.

◆ Whether the performer wears one persona per course, the faculty member's own licensed likeness, or a face the student chooses from an approved set is a governance call before it is a technical one. It is waiting for you in the open questions.

VII.
The Programme

The room gets smarter
by phase.

The product moves in your sequence: deck to script to video first, standalone first. What grows phase over phase is the data room the council reasons from.

PHASE I · NOW
The deck is the data room
One faculty deck, one non-quantitative course, standalone. Chunks, scripts, faculty review, narrated video. Every claim traced or tagged. The harness does not need to be good yet. It needs to be honest.
The room holds: your slides
PHASE II
The course joins the room
LTI 1.3 into Moodle brings the course shell: syllabus, prior modules, assessment structure. Live regional job-market context connects concepts to practice. Arabic arrives as a performance dimension, in step with the national agenda.
The room holds: the course, the region
PHASE III
The ensemble turns upstream
The council critiques the incoming deck itself against learning design principles before production begins, and learning signals from real cohorts feed the next rehearsal. Your standards agenda, encoded and running.
The room holds: how students actually learned

VIII.
Where the Engine Stops

Left to the faculty's
judgment.

The room is built to know what it does not own. These calls are surfaced, named, and handed over. Deliberately.

◆ Calls the council deliberately leaves to the faculty member
“
Is it true? Disciplinary accuracy is your lane, named as yours in the interface. The room checks structure, alignment, and provenance. It does not pretend to outrank you on the discipline itself.
“
Is it taught the way you would teach it? Taste, emphasis, and the choice of example are conducting decisions. The room proposes; the podium is yours.
“
How bold should this chunk be? The Provocateur argues for the uncomfortable question. Whether your room is ready for it is a judgment no model should make.
“
What gets cut? A score is also made of what it leaves out. The omissions are yours to own, with the room's reasoning open in front of you.

What we are not sure about,

A draft that cannot be argued with is not a draft. These are the questions we want this document to start.

We propose a Management mind, in your own discipline, where your eye is sharpest. Which course, and whose deck, should sit first?

iv.

Dubai or Australia. The answer sets the LTI approval path and the Phase II timeline.
v.
What must the pilot prove?
Hours returned to faculty, completion, satisfaction, or something you watch that we have not named.
vi.
Does assessment eventually join the ensemble?
Your public argument is that assessment must be reinvented toward demonstrated competence. Is that a future seat in this room, or a different room entirely?
vii.
Whose face holds the lectern?
One persona per course, the faculty member's own licensed likeness, or a face the student chooses from an approved set. Consent, identity, and institutional policy travel with the answer.
viii.
How far may an interlude wander?
The performer answers inside the score and the deck. The latitude beyond that edge, and the moment it must say ask your professor, is a dial for you to set, not for us to assume.
